Freckers
Each quadrant must have numbers 1 through 9 without duplicates.

There can be no duplicate numbers in any row or column.

Every SuDoKu board has exactly one correct combination of numbers, and all spaces must be filled to complete a board.

When all boards are complete...you win!

There are 2 modes for interacting with a SuDoKu board.

Answer Mode is the pencil icon (activated by clicking flag button) and any number highlighted will be the number you enter into a cell when you click there.

There other mode for interacting with a SuDoKu board is Flag Mode.

Flag Mode is the button with a flag icon (activated by clicking pencil button) and any number highlighted will be the number you enter into a cell when you click there.

This has no effect on the outcome of a game, but can help you keep track of your options.

Each level of Sudoku Extreme increases the number of boards in play exponentially: 1, 9, 81, 729, 6561, ...

Build time increases significantly for each level chosen as all boards are interconnected.

With the exception of the highest level,
each board's quadrant is associated with an entire checkers board in the level above it.

Since there are 9 quadrants to a board, each board has 9 additional boards associated with it in the level above.
Each cell on a board in a lower level connects to the center cell of a quadrant within a board one level higher.

Enter a number in a lower level board, and the upper level board will be changed. The reverse is also true.

The board navigation menu lets you hop through boards connected to each other level by level.

When on level 1's board, the buttons beneath are the boards connected to it. Click on button 3 in the next level to see that board, and also get buttons 20 through 28, which are the boards connected to it, and in turn connected to the board on level 1.

Click on button 3 to go back to that board, or the level 1 button to go back to that board.

When playing of screens with 768px or higher, an additional helper is available.

When hovering over the board, the three quadrants in the hovered over column reveal their associated boards in the right-hand-side display.

The specific quadrant hovered over will highlight that specific board in blue. The hovered over cell will also highlight the connected cell with a peach color.

When playing at higher levels, you probably won't have the time to finish the game in a single sitting.

To account for this, SuDoKu ExTrEmE allows you to save your game. Simply click on the "Save" button.

Either copy the long string of alphanumerical characters manually, or you can click either the copy to clipboard button, or the download to text file button.

Now that you have your game's state saved in a key, you can paste it into the load game section. You can find this on the start menu by clicking the "Load Game" button.

Once pasted, simply press the green button in the upper corner and your previous game will populate the screen.
